Notable restaurants

Indiana like many other States has built a food culture that will attract even the most
hard core foodies. Whether you're in the State Capital Indianapolis or one of the smaller
charming cities or towns there are certainly options for you. Below are some of the more notable
places to eat.

9th Street Bistro

A restaurant located in downtown Noblesville Indiana, 9th Street Bistro is run by a married duo
who brought there culinary talents from Denver to Indiana. With an award winning chef in the kitchen
the concept is "Globally-Inspired Bistro fare". The food is thought up from their own world travels
and made up of local ingredients whenever possible.

Bluebeard

BLuebeard started as an artisinal bakery that grew into an award winning restaurant. Opened in 2012
it quickly became an Indianapolis hotspot. Inspired by Indianas own Kurt Vonneguts book of the same title
Bluebeard is known for a modern take on midwestern cuisine. Its a James Beard Award semi-finalist restaurant
for Best New Restaurants in America.

Cafe Navarre

Located in South Bend Indiana Cafe Navarre is a locally owned establishment. It serves modern
American cuisine with European influences. At Navarre you you can find prime steaks, seafood,
fresh fish, with an assortment of seasonal local flavors.

Adams Street Chophouse

Located in the historic Thomas S. Neely House in Muncie Indiana, this upscale restraunt wants its diners to feel
as though they have stepped into the roaring twenties. It has themed rooms such as the 1920's Adams St Room and Speakeasy,
the fare consists of typical chophouse selections as well as some midwestern delights.

Essence Restaurant

A new restraunt in Elkhard Indiana, essence strives to serve a mouth watering experience. Focused on high class dining
you can expect an assortment of different cuisines at Essence. Reservations at this time are encouraged as walk-in seating
cannot be guarenteed.
